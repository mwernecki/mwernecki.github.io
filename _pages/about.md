I am a microbiologist with an interest in computer science. My theoretical framework is grounded in biological concepts of adaptation and variation, such as population variability, and this is usually my starting point when analysing biological problems. My second anchor is model thinking and statistical modelling, of which I am a big fan, though I am still relatively a beginner.

My lab skills are a solid set typical for general microbiology needs plus molecular biology methods: some classics like cloning and PCR, and state-of-the-art like nanopore sequencing (of bacterial genomes and metagenomes, at this point). Beside that I do tinkering when my research needs custom solutions. I find 3D printing and robotic automation really useful for this. It's all DIY and amateurish, but it works.

I have been connected to the Department of Microbiology at the University of Wrocław since my high school project, then as a student. And finally, it's where I'm preparing my PhD thesis (and got involved in plenty of other projects). However, during this time, I have had multiple opportunities to get more diverse experience in academia and industry, including internships in Belgian, Spanish and German institutions. I also studied in a 1-year postgraduate programme in *data science* at Warsaw University of Technology, to get some formal IT education. Which paid off with much better programming skills and my general readiness to do, well, data science.

I also teach microbiology at the University of Wrocław. My courses cover general bacteriology, molecular bacteriology, and industrial microbiology. In the latter, I experiment with a transdisciplinary approach to address the problems of the circular economy and demonstrate its deep connection with microbiology.
